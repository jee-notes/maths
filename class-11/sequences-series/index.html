Sequences & Series

Understanding mathematical patterns, discrete progression, and cumulative sums.

Why This Chapter Matters

Sequences provide the foundational framework for analyzing patterns in mathematics. While functions map continuous domains to ranges, sequences represent discrete mappings from natural numbers. Series represent the cumulative total of these discrete mappings.

This chapter is a critical transition to advanced calculus, setting the stage for Taylor series, integration concepts, and infinite limits. Understanding how numbers behave cumulatively when structured under specific algebraic rules is essential.

For the JEE, this topic is less about finding the 10th term and more about mastering algebraic manipulation, telescoping sums, and utilizing inequalities like AM-GM to identify bounds and extremums in highly complex expressions.

Scope of This Chapter

This chapter clarifies:

  • Arithmetic Progressions (A.P.) & Arithmetic Means
  • Geometric Progressions (G.P.) & Infinite Geometric Series
  • Harmonic Progressions (H.P.)
  • The AM-GM-HM Inequality and its applications
  • Arithmetico-Geometric Progressions (A.G.P.)
  • Special Series, Sigma Notation, and the Method of Differences
